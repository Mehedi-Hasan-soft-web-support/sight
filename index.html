ü§ù What is IEEE SIGHT?

IEEE Special Interest Group on Humanitarian Technology (SIGHT) is a global network of IEEE volunteers partnering with underserved communities and local organizations to leverage technology for sustainable development and improved quality of life.

üí≠ Why This Competition?

We believe in empowering students to create meaningful change through innovation. This competition provides a platform for aspiring changemakers to develop and pitch technology-driven solutions that address real-world social challenges.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Can first-year students participate? +
Yes! The competition is open to all DIU students regardless of their year of study. We encourage first-year students to participate and showcase their innovative ideas.
Is there any registration fee? +
No, the competition is completely free to participate. There are no registration or submission fees.
Can one person submit multiple ideas? +
No, each participant or team can submit only one idea. However, you can refine and improve your submission before the deadline.
Do we need a working prototype? +
No, a working prototype is not mandatory. You need to submit a well-thought-out idea with clear problem statement, proposed solution, and expected impact. However, having a prototype or proof of concept will strengthen your submission.
Can team members be from different departments? +
Yes! Cross-departmental teams are encouraged as they bring diverse perspectives and skills to solve complex problems.
